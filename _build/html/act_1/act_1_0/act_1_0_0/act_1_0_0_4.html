Vincent will play a pivotal role in not just data analysis but also in guiding the research methodologies of analysts, students, and residents. He will have my ear, thereby directly influencing the direction of anything I’m involved in over the coming months and year. Diana Call and Rita Johnson-Chase have been instrumental in managing the hiring process.

Given Vincent’s integral role and the fact that he will be available to all members of the department daily, it is of the utmost urgency that we allocate office space for him if at all it is possible—hopefully by next week. This will enable immediate integration into our current projects and workflow.

Big Picture: Introduction of the Fena Platform

To further enhance our work, I have developed a platform, Fena, aimed at optimizing clinical research workflows. This initiative will directly benefit everyone in TRC by addressing challenges like rigor, error management, and effective code integration. Vincent will also be deeply involved in rolling out this platform to the team.

I would greatly appreciate your prompt attention to the immediate allocation of office space for Vincent. Additionally, I am eager to discuss the broader strategic plan involving Fena and would welcome your feedback. I am more than happy to arrange a meeting in the coming weeks to go over the details.

Thank you for your time and consideration. Your prompt action on these matters will be greatly beneficial for the future of our TRC.

Best regards,

Abi

SBIR Phase I Proposal for NIA#

Title: AI-Driven Risk Calculator for Evaluating Perioperative and Long-Term Risks in Older Live Kidney Donors#

Principal Investigator: Abimereki Muzaale, MD, MPH, PhD Candidate#

Affiliation: Johns Hopkins University#

Email: muzaale@jhmi.edu#

Phone: (240) 281-3154#

Abstract#

We aim to develop an Artificial Intelligence (AI)-based risk calculator that predicts perioperative and long-term risks in older adults (aged ≥ 55) who are potential live kidney donors. This tool will assist healthcare providers in making data-driven decisions during donor selection and will provide evidence-based insights for donor-provider discussions. Since we already have a supervised model of a similar risk calculator, we will use this as a starting point and augment it with additional data. We will then validate the model using existing datasets and prospective data. Finally, we will develop a user-friendly interface for healthcare providers. Upon successful development, the tool will be made available to healthcare institutions as a Software as a Service (SaaS) model.

Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle and the bias-variance tradeoff are two sides of the same coin. The former is a physical law, while the latter is a mathematical principle. Both are fundamental to the human condition and are inextricably linked to the road chosen by Robert Frost. morality, the well-trodden path, will yield precise but biased results. amorality, the road less traveled, will yield unbiased but imprecise results.

ones tolerance for uncertainty is inversely proportional to ones tolerance for bias, and is likely to result in one trecking the yellow flow zone in robert frost’s yellow wood.

Certainly, a step-by-step process can be an effective way to achieve a specific goal, whether it’s in research, starting a tech business, or exploring complex concepts like inflection control curves (ICCs). Here’s a generalized guide that could be tailored to your specific projects:

For Research on ICCs:#

Step 1: Literature Review#

  • Review existing research on ICCs across disciplines. Identify gaps in knowledge and questions unanswered.

Step 2: Formulate Hypotheses#

  • Develop hypotheses based on the gaps identified. Prepare a research proposal that includes methods and expected outcomes.

Step 3: Seek Funding#

  • Apply for grants (NIH, NSF, etc.), consult program officers, and look for internal funding if applicable.

Step 4: Assemble Team#

  • Recruit team members with expertise in relevant fields like statistics, arts, and data science.

Step 5: Data Collection#

  • Design and conduct experiments, or compile existing data.

Step 6: Data Analysis#

  • Use random effects models and other appropriate statistical techniques.

Step 7: Publish Findings#

  • Write a research paper and seek publication in relevant journals.

Step 8: Publicize#

  • Share your research through seminars, online platforms, and academic conferences.

For Tech Startups:#

Step 1: Market Research#

  • Validate your idea for an educational tech startup by conducting market research.

Step 2: Business Plan#

  • Draft a business plan, which includes a financial model and marketing strategy.

Step 3: Register Business#

  • Register your startup as a small business entity.

Step 4: Secure Funding#

  • Look for SBIR/STTR grants, venture capital, or crowdfunding.

Step 5: Build MVP (Minimum Viable Product)#

  • Develop a prototype or MVP.

Step 6: Test and Iterate#

  • Launch to a small audience, gather feedback, and iterate.

Step 7: Scale#

  • After refining the product, look to scale through partnerships, additional funding, or marketing campaigns.

For Exploring Dramatic Arcs in ICCs:#

Step 1: Conceptualize#

  • Define what inflection points in ICCs mean in both arts and sciences.

Step 2: Research#

  • Explore existing theories that discuss turning points or inflection points in other contexts.

Step 3: Develop Model#

  • Create a theoretical model that could be tested.

Step 4: Interdisciplinary Collaboration#

  • Partner with experts in both arts and sciences to gain broader insights.

Step 5: Publish and Present#

  • Share your findings through publications and presentations.

Next Steps:#

  1. Immediate Objectives: Prioritize one or two projects and allocate resources accordingly. Formulate a timeline for each step.

  2. Long-term Objectives: Keep a vision for how these projects feed into your long-term career goals.

  3. Resource Allocation: Consider the time and financial investments for each step. Forge cross-departmental collaborations to pool resources and expertise.

You’re well-placed to handle the complexities these projects may entail. By breaking down each into manageable steps, you can strategically plan your way forward.
